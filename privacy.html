Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 9, 2026

At Horology Press LLC ("we", "us", or "our"), safeguarding the privacy of our visitors and customers is of paramount importance. This Privacy Policy document outlines the types of personal information collected, recorded, and processed when you visit or make a purchase from our website.

1. Information We Collect

When you purchase a digital license or contact us via our site, we collect specific personal identifiers to fulfill your delivery. This includes:

  • Personal details: Full Name, Billing Address, and Email Address.
  • Transactional information: Record of items purchased, price paid, and transaction reference numbers.
  • Technical Metadata: IP address, device type, browser information, and referral page patterns collected through standard analytical tools.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your data strictly to execute transactions and manage customer relationships:

  • To generate your unique eBook access links and distribute them immediately upon checkout.
  • To issue receipts, transaction records, and invoice documentation required by financial merchant processors.
  • To answer your support inquiries and provide assistance on technical formatting challenges.
  • To comply with regulatory audit requirements and combat fraudulent card actions.

Note on Payment Details: All payment transactions are completed through secure third-party merchant servers (Visa, MasterCard, American Express). We do not view, process, or store your credit card digits on our hosting platforms. All credit card details are encrypted using Secure Socket Layer (SSL) protocols.

3. Cookies and Analytical Tools

We utilize standard cookies to analyze traffic patterns and maintain temporary sessions. Cookies are micro-files saved on your computer memory that remember preferences. You can adjust your browser properties to block cookies, though some interactive elements on our checkout page may not operate optimally as a result.

4. Data Protection & Security

We employ comprehensive mechanical, electronic, and managerial procedures to safeguard customer information. Despite these advanced protocols, no digital transmission across the Internet can be guaranteed as 100% secure. In the rare event of a data risk, we will notify affected parties and regulatory bodies immediately in accordance with state laws.

5. Your Data Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • The right to request a digital record of the personal data we hold.
  • The right to demand correction of inaccurate personal identifiers.
  • The right to request absolute deletion of your personal records from our systems, except where transactional records are mandated for legal tax compliance.
